# Pricing Strategy

A structured approach to pricing inventory to balance profitability, sell-through speed, and brand positioning.

---

## Objective

To price items in a way that:
- Maximizes sell-through within 30–45 days  
- Maintains strong margins  
- Supports a premium, curated brand perception  

---

## Core Pricing Model

Target resale pricing:

- 40%–70% of original retail value  
- Adjust based on brand, condition, and demand  

---

## Cost-to-Price Ratio

Minimum targets:

- Low-end items: 2.5x cost  
- Standard items: 3x–4x cost  
- High-demand items: 4x–5x cost  

If this is not achievable → do not buy or reprice lower for quick movement.

---

## Pricing Tiers

### Tier 1: High Demand / Premium
- Strong brands  
- Timeless or trending styles  
- Excellent condition  

**Pricing Strategy:**
- Price at higher end (60–70% retail)  
- Minimal discounting  

---

### Tier 2: Standard / Everyday
- Recognizable brands  
- Good, wearable styles  

**Pricing Strategy:**
- Mid-range pricing (45–60% retail)  
- Expect moderate markdown  

---

### Tier 3: Slow Movers
- Lower-demand brands  
- Less versatile items  

**Pricing Strategy:**
- Price to sell quickly  
- Bundle or discount early  

---

## Markdown Strategy

### Timeline

- Day 0–14: Full price  
- Day 15–30: 10–20% markdown  
- Day 30–45: 20–40% markdown or bundle  
- Day 45+: Clear out (bundle, discount, or remove)

---

## Bundling Strategy

Use when:
- Items are lower value  
- Individual pieces are not strong sellers  

Examples:
- “2 for $30”  
- “3 for $40”  
- Outfit bundles  

---

## Psychological Pricing

- Use rounded, approachable pricing ($18, $24, $32)  
- Avoid overly complex numbers  
- Price to feel like a “find”  

---

## Pricing Rules

- Price for movement, not perfection  
- Do not overprice average items  
- Adjust quickly based on performance  

---

## Performance Feedback Loop

Track:
- What sells quickly  
- What sits  
- What requires markdown  

Use this data to refine:
- Future buying decisions  
- Pricing tiers  
- Brand acceptance  

---

## Summary

Profit is driven by:
- Smart buying  
- Strategic pricing  
- Fast sell-through  

Not by holding out for the highest possible price.
