# Buying Checklist

A standardized framework for evaluating inventory before purchase to ensure quality, sell-through potential, and margin viability.

---

## Objective

To make consistent, profitable buying decisions by removing emotion and applying clear, repeatable criteria.

---

## Core Rule

If it does not meet **at least 2 of the 3** below, do not buy:

- Recognizable brand  
- Current or timeless style  
- Easy to style / wearable  

---

## Step 1: Brand Evaluation

### Accept
- Known, searchable brands  
- Premium or mid-tier brands with consistent demand  

### Caution
- Mall brands (select only if style is strong)  
- Boutique/unknown brands (only if standout piece)  

### Avoid
- No-label or fast fashion with no differentiation  
- Overly saturated brands unless style is exceptional  

---

## Step 2: Style & Relevance

Ask:
- Does this look current or timeless?  
- Would someone wear this today without styling effort?  
- Can I immediately think of how to style this?  

### Strong Indicators
- Neutral colors  
- Classic silhouettes  
- Elevated basics  
- Versatile layering pieces  

### Avoid
- Outdated cuts or prints  
- Overly specific or hard-to-style items  
- Occasion-only pieces (unless high value)  

---

## Step 3: Condition Check

Only accept items in excellent condition.

### Must Have
- No stains  
- No holes  
- No excessive wear or pilling  
- Functional zippers, buttons, closures  

### Exception
- High-value brands may allow minor flaws (priced accordingly)

---

## Step 4: Size Consideration

All sizes are acceptable, including extended sizes.

Evaluate based on:
- Fit appeal  
- Style relevance  
- Demand potential  

Note:
Extended sizes are underserved and can be a strategic advantage when styled well.

---

## Step 5: Profitability Check

Before purchasing, confirm:

- Can this reasonably sell for 2.5x–5x cost?  
- Is there clear demand for this item type?  
- Will this move within 30–45 days?  

If unsure → pass or negotiate lower price.

---

## Step 6: Final Filter

Ask:

- Would I buy this again?  
- Would I feature this in content?  
- Does this fit the Lovelist brand aesthetic?  

If hesitation exists → do not buy.

---

## Buying Rules

- Do not buy based on volume alone  
- Do not buy to “fill inventory”  
- Do not buy sight unseen  

---

## Summary

The goal is not to acquire more inventory.

The goal is to acquire **better inventory**.
